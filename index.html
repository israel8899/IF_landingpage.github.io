Israel First – Our Plan to Put Citizens First

Putting Israel and its citizens first: jobs, homes, security, and sovereignty.

1. Domestic Labor & Employment

The backbone of Israel is its people. Big corporations and bought politicians flood our country with foreign labor, driving down wages and taking jobs from Israelis. Enough.

  • Phase out foreign labor in sectors where Israelis are available within five years.
  • Create 200,000 new domestic jobs through vocational training, apprenticeships, and industrial development.
  • Launch a Young Immigrant Workforce Initiative: attract 50,000 young immigrants to join the labor force in the first three years.
  • Provide tax incentives for businesses that maintain 80%+ Israeli staff; penalize wage suppression through foreign labor.

2. Housing & Generational Wealth

Young Israelis work hard, pay rent, and still cannot afford to own a home. Meanwhile, the powerful hoard land and build overpriced apartments.

  • Build 20,000 single-family homes in sprawling new communities across Israel & Judea & Samaria.
  • Grant long-term land rights for citizens in new development zones.
  • Launch a National Housing Fund for first-time buyers with subsidized mortgage rates.
  • Ensure transparent tenders and anti-speculation policies to prevent price inflation.

3. Economic Independence & Industrial Growth

  • Invest in Israeli manufacturing, agriculture, defense technologies, and industrial development.
  • Revive the Israeli jet fighter development program.
  • Provide grants and low-interest loans to Israeli startups creating domestic jobs.
  • Expand domestic agriculture to reduce dependency on imported goods.

4. National Sovereignty & Territorial Integration

  • Dismantle the Palestinian Authority; establish a single governing authority under Israel from the Jordan River to the Mediterranean.
  • Prioritize strategic land development for national security and population integration.
  • Build rail networks and highways connecting all regions, including Judea, Samaria, Negev, and Galilee.
  • Evaluate the Ben Gurion Canal as a long-term national strategic asset.

5. National Security & Defense Innovation

  • Fund development of an Israeli-built advanced jet fighter.
  • Expand defense technology R&D including cyber, unmanned systems, and missile defense.
  • Strengthen domestic military manufacturing for self-reliance in critical defense systems.
  • Implement mandatory national defense coordination for critical infrastructure.

6. Geopolitical Independence & Democratic Alliances

  • Diversify diplomatic and trade relations to reduce reliance on any single foreign power.
  • Lead the creation of a Democratic Alliance of Nations: democracies coordinate on security, economic policy, and global diplomacy.
  • Exclude authoritarian regimes from influencing this alliance.
  • Develop bilateral partnerships with emerging powers for technology transfer and defense collaboration.

7. Anti-Corruption & Transparency

  • Establish an independent auditing authority to examine all ministries, public bodies, and state-funded entities.
  • Mandate public disclosure of foreign donations, contracts, and influence in government decision-making.
  • Introduce whistleblower protections for employees reporting corruption or undue foreign influence.

8. National Unity & Social Cohesion

  • Encourage civic programs that bridge secular, religious, urban, and rural communities.
  • Implement youth and veteran engagement programs linking national service to employment and education benefits.
  • Promote community integration initiatives to foster mutual respect and shared responsibility.

9. Infrastructure & Connectivity

  • Develop railways and highways connecting all regions for economic and social mobility.
  • Expand water, energy, and communications infrastructure to support population growth and industrial development.
  • Prioritize infrastructure projects that create domestic jobs and foster national resilience.
